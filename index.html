about_me.txt

I am Vanessa, a multimedia designer/creator Focus on building digital and interactive experiences that help people. My main interest is in designing apps, games, and accessible digital tools that support people with ADHD, neurodivergence, and different cognitive needs, while still feeling thoughtful, human, and visually engaging.

what_i_do.txt

I work across different mediums, but they all serve the same goal: creating experiences that are useful, inclusive, and emotionally aware. Whether I am working through UX, web, game development, visual storytelling, or research, I am always trying to understand how design can reduce frustration, support autonomy, and make people feel more understood.

my_focus.txt

I am especially interested in cognitive accessibility, neurodiversity, inclusive design, and interactive experiences with social impact. I want to create digital products that are not only functional, but also more humane, more intuitive, and more respectful of the different ways people think, feel, and move through the world.

philosophy.txt

I care about design that helps, not just design that looks good. For me, creativity is a way of solving real problems, and I am most motivated by projects that can improve comfort, confidence, usability, and quality of life for people who are often overlooked by mainstream design.

📂 accolades_archive_payload.sys

First Prize Winner — Multicultural Event Poster Contest (Luxembourg)

As a teenager, I made a hand-drawn poster for a multicultural event in Luxembourg. I almost did not submit it because I did not think it was good enough, especially since I did not have digital tools and the drawing itself felt simple to me at the time. My teacher encouraged me to send it anyway, and I ended up winning first prize. The poster was later displayed across the city, including on buses, and it was especially meaningful to see something I had doubted become part of the public space.

What stayed with me most was not just the award, but the reminder that trying can matter even when you do not fully believe in your own work yet.
