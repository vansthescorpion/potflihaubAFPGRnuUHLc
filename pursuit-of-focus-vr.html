Project File #6 — Virtual reality game

In Pursuit of Focus

A first-person VR ADHD simulator that translates distraction, working-memory limits, and executive dysfunction into gameplay, making cognitive overload experiential rather than purely explained.

Overview & Goal Strategy

In Pursuit of Focus is a first-person VR game designed as an ADHD simulator. Its goal is to let players experience aspects of ADHD through interaction, especially limited working memory, involuntary distraction, time blindness, and executive dysfunction, rather than learning about them only through explanation. The project aims to build understanding through embodied gameplay. Instead of presenting ADHD as a list of symptoms, it uses mechanics that make players feel how attention can be hijacked, how simple tasks can become unstable, and how cognitive overload can interfere with action.

Scenario Parameters & Mechanics

The game places the player in a time-pressured environment with a clear objective and a short deadline. In the documented version, the player must clean a shared dorm room before a roommate and her mother arrive, turning a simple goal into a struggle shaped by distraction, memory strain, and task overload. The game is built around a three-slot working-memory system, where only three active tasks can be held at once. Looking at a fourth task pushes an older one out, while involuntary distractions can hijack the task list entirely and lock the player into irrelevant but compelling actions.

Executive Dysfunction Simulation

The project also represents executive dysfunction through interface and interaction glitches. When the task list is full, choice paralysis is simulated by making the completion button harder to select, and certain context switches can trigger memory wipes that clear the task list entirely, forcing the player to reconstruct what they were doing. Players are not simply told that working memory is limited or that distractions are hard to resist — they are placed inside systems where attention shifts automatically, tasks disappear from mental reach, and progress becomes harder the more overwhelmed they feel.

Sensory Language & Critical Reflection

The game uses narrowed field of view, audio masking, flickering tasks, and visual decay to communicate hyperfocus, overwhelm, and forgetting. These elements help transform cognitive strain into something the player can sense through the body and interface, not only through words. An important part of the project is its own self-critique. The report reflects on how framing ADHD through a messy-room scenario can reinforce stereotypes around laziness or disorganization, and it acknowledges that a more accurate future version should go beyond obvious distractions and simplistic assumptions. This project is important to my practice because it brings together interactive design, empathy-building, and neurodiversity. It shows my interest in creating digital experiences that do not just present information, but actively reshape how people understand cognitive difference through participation. In Pursuit of Focus explores how VR can be used not only for immersion, but also for empathy, making invisible cognitive friction perceptible through play.
