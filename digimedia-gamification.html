Gamification app concept

Milho a Pardais

A gamified UI/UX concept for the University of Aveiro that explores how research, interface design, and motivation can work together to make student interaction more engaging and rewarding.

The Problem

The main challenge was that the existing experience lacked strong engagement and did not clearly motivate users to interact with the platform. The project aimed to understand what discouraged participation, what made the journey less intuitive, and how gamification could make the experience feel more meaningful and rewarding.

The Solution

The proposed solution was a gamification-based app concept supported by a structured UI/UX process. Research findings were used to define pain points and opportunities, then translated into interface concepts, user flows, and prototypes that balanced usability with playful motivation.

Methodology & Breakdown

Overview: Milho a Pardais is a gamified app concept created for the University of Aveiro to explore how a more engaging digital experience could improve student interaction with academic or campus-related content. The project combined user research, form-based data collection, interface design, and iterative prototyping to define a solution grounded in real user needs.

Process Framework: The work followed a user-centred process similar to the Double Diamond model. It moved from research and analysis to design and evaluation: collecting data through forms, reviewing literature, identifying patterns, building information architecture, sketching wireframes, developing interface concepts, and refining the prototype through feedback.

Metrics & Outcomes: The final outcome was a clearer project direction, a gamified app concept, and a UI prototype shaped by user research. The project showed how visual design, interaction, and gamification can work together to improve engagement when they are based on real insight rather than decoration.

Key Takeaways: This project confirmed that gamification is most effective when it responds to actual user behaviour and motivation. It also reinforced the value of a structured design process, because each decision stayed connected to research rather than being made only for visual effect.

Why it matters: Milho a Pardais fits well in my portfolio because it combines research, interface design, and concept development in a way that reflects my interest in human-centred digital experiences. It also connects to my broader focus on accessible, thoughtful, and motivating design for real users. Milho a Pardais explores how research-driven gamification can transform a platform from something users simply access into something they actively want to engage with.
